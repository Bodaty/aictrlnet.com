## The Three-Layer Automation Reach

Every automation platform has the same problem: they only work with what's in their integrations catalog. Miss one system, and the whole workflow breaks. You're back to copy-pasting between tabs.

We solved this with three layers of reach — each one expanding where AI can operate.

### Layer 1: Platform Adapters — 10,000+ Tools

Business Edition includes native adapters for five major automation platforms: n8n, Zapier, Make, IFTTT, and Power Automate. Each of these platforms connects to thousands of apps. Through a single adapter, your AI agent gains access to their entire ecosystem.

That's not 36 integrations. That's 36 adapters — each one a gateway to hundreds or thousands of tools. n8n alone has 400+ integrations. Zapier has 7,000+. Make has 1,500+. Your AI agent doesn't need separate connectors for each one — it speaks to the platform, and the platform speaks to everything else.

The n8n adapter was tested with 6/6 scenarios passing in live integration testing. Workflows trigger, data flows, results return — all orchestrated by your AI agent, all governed by the same policy engine that controls everything else in AICtrlNet.

### Layer 2: Self-Extending Agents — Any API

What about the system that isn't in any catalog? The internal API your team built? The niche CRM from 2019?

Self-extending agents handle it. When your AI agent encounters a service it doesn't have an adapter for, it doesn't stop and file a feature request. It builds the integration on the spot — securely validated, governed by the same policies as everything else in AICtrlNet. Low-risk integrations activate automatically. Higher-risk ones route to a human for approval.

It works with simple APIs and complex ones alike — pagination, OAuth, custom logic. All of it.

```
User: "Pull our latest deals from AcmeCRM"

Agent: "I don't have an AcmeCRM adapter yet. Give me a moment..."
Agent: "Done. You have 23 open deals — $1.2M pipeline. Here's the breakdown..."
```

Minutes, not months. No developer needed.

### Layer 3: Browser Automation — Any Web App

No API at all? Legacy system with only a web interface? Government portal? Internal tool that hasn't been updated since 2015?

Browser automation drives it. AI navigation that operates any web application like a human would — clicking, typing, reading, extracting data from screens. Your AI agent navigates the UI, fills forms, downloads reports, and extracts the data your workflow needs. All governed by the same ALLOW/DENY/ESCALATE framework as every other action.

This is the layer that closes every remaining gap. The vendor with no API? Automated. The compliance portal that only works in Internet Explorer? Automated. The internal tool that the original developer left three years ago? Automated.

**The result: if it exists, we can automate it.**

Platform adapters for the 10,000+ tools with public integrations. Self-extending agents for any API. Browser automation for everything else. Three layers, zero gaps.

### AI Governance Framework

Every AI action flows through the governance framework:

- **ALLOW**: Action proceeds automatically
- **DENY**: Action is blocked with explanation
- **ESCALATE**: Action is routed to a human for approval

Policies are configurable per workflow, per department, per user role. Intelligent risk scoring evaluates every action in real time. Full audit trails capture who did what, when, and why.

This is what lets the CTO say yes. Not because AI is limited — because AI is accountable. Every decision has an audit trail. Every action has a policy. Every exception has a human in the loop.

Governance isn't the thing that slows AI down. It's the thing that lets you deploy AI in the first place. The CTO who can't explain what the AI did last Tuesday doesn't approve the project. The CTO who can pull up a full audit trail in 30 seconds signs off with confidence.

## The Control Spectrum: Your Pace

AI automation isn't binary — "fully manual" or "fully autonomous" is a false choice. The Control Spectrum gives you six phases of autonomy, configurable per department, per workflow, per user role.

| Phase | Mode | What Happens |
|-------|------|-------------|
| 1 | **Manual** | AI suggests, human does everything |
| 2 | **Assistance** | AI drafts, human reviews and approves |
| 3 | **Supervised** | AI executes, human monitors and can intervene |
| 4 | **Delegated** | AI handles routine, humans handle exceptions |
| 5 | **Autonomous** | AI operates independently with governance guardrails |
| 6 | **Full Automation** | AI manages end-to-end, humans review outcomes |

Community Edition gives you Phases 1-2. Business Edition unlocks Phases 3-6.

Start where you're comfortable. Your marketing team might be at Phase 4 — AI autonomously runs campaign workflows, humans review results. Your finance team might be at Phase 2 — AI drafts expense reports and reconciliations, humans approve every one. Your customer service team might be at Phase 5 — AI handles tier-1 tickets end-to-end, humans handle escalations.

That's fine — that's the point. Every department has different risk tolerance, different regulatory requirements, different comfort levels. The Control Spectrum respects all of them. Grow when you're ready, not when a vendor tells you to.

## Why Now

Three things happened in February that made this launch feel overdue rather than early:

**1. OpenAI backed autonomous agents.** OpenClaw's creator joined OpenAI. The project is becoming a foundation. Corporate-backed autonomous agents are the new default. Governance isn't optional anymore.

**2. The data got alarming.** Microsoft found 80% of Fortune 500 companies running AI agents. Gravitee found only 14.4% have security approval for those agents. 88% have confirmed or suspected incidents. The gap between adoption and governance is a crisis, not a trend.

**3. The market validated governance as a category.** Proofpoint acquired Acuvity for AI agent governance. The Agentic AI Foundation formed under the Linux Foundation. When acquisitions and foundations happen, the category is real.

We didn't rush to announce. We wanted to ship first, announce second. The platform has been running — 171 tools, 29 adapters, 183 templates, 43 agents, 211 functional tests passing. Now we're making it official.
